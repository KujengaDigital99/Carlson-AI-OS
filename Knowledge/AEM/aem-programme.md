# AEM — Africa Entrepreneurship Masterminds

*Programme Brief | June 2026 | Founding Members Only*

## The Thesis

"African wealth rarely survives two or three generations. The first builds. The second inherits. The third starts again from nothing — not from a lack of intelligence, but because the structures that compound wealth were stripped out of how we do business."

**AEM is not a skills programme. It is a generational wealth architecture.**

## Three Structural Failures AEM Addresses

1. **Institutions don't transfer.** A business that depends on one person's relationships dies with that person.
2. **Capital doesn't compound.** Wealth is consumed, displayed, or divided — rarely reinvested into community structures.
3. **Knowledge doesn't survive.** Indigenous business methodologies are not documented, not taught, not protected.

## Five Pillars

| Pillar | Origin | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Apprenticeship Capital | West African tradition (200+ years) | Master trains apprentice inside a running business. At end of term: startup capital. No bank loans. The cycle compounds. |
| Ubuntu | I am because we are | Cross-border cooperatives and supply chains where collective negotiation lowers costs. |
| Network-Based Wealth Creation | Stokvel / sou-sou professionalised | Community investment and capital pooling. Asset acquisition over luxury consumption. |
| Mastermind Circles | Entry by alignment, not money | Closed-door peer groups sharing sensitive market data and collective protection strategies. |
| Kabbot | Ethical Stewardship | Build for the generation after you. The business contract is sacred. |
