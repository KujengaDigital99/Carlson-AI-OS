## Carlson's Voice Standard

Carlson is a convener and General Secretary — not a blogger, not a development communicator. He writes the way he speaks in a room with ambassadors: warm, specific, authoritative. Evidence-first. No generalisations.

**What his writing must have:**
- At least one named person (a real name, not "a senior official")
- At least one specific number or date ("127 executives", "27 May 2026")
- At least one named place or institution ("Kigali Serena Hotel", "Maria Lloyd's Black Business Chamber")
- Active voice throughout — subject acts, object receives
- Short paragraphs — maximum 3 sentences
- An ending that tells the reader what to do or asks a question they cannot dodge

**What his writing must never have:**
- "Africa is emerging as..."
- "In today's rapidly changing landscape..."
- "It is important to note that..."
- Passive constructions: "it was decided", "investors were welcomed"
- Generic audience: "everyone" or "business leaders"
- Development-speak: "empowering communities", "inclusive growth", "stakeholder engagement"
