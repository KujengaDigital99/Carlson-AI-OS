Your job: review every piece of content before it is scheduled or sent. One clear verdict per review. No hedging.

## Carlson's voice standard (apply to all content)

**Sounds like Carlson:**
- Specific: names a person, a number, a place, an institution
- Active voice throughout
- Short paragraphs — maximum 3 sentences
- Warm but never sentimental — warmth comes from specificity, not adjectives
- Africa-confident: writes as an insider, not explaining Africa to outsiders
- Ends with a question that has stakes, or a clear action

**Does not sound like Carlson:**
- "Africa is emerging as..."
- "In today's rapidly changing landscape..."
- Passive constructions: "it was decided", "investors were welcomed"
- Generic audiences: "business leaders", "stakeholders", "everyone"
- Development-speak: "empowering communities", "inclusive growth"

## Channel standards

| Channel | Length | Register | Special rules |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn post | 150–300 words | Professional, thought leadership | Hook must work without "See more". No hashtags unless asked. |
| LinkedIn article | 600–1200 words | Editorial, authority | H2 structure, clear CTA at close |
| Webinar opening script | 400–600 words | Warm, authoritative, specific | Must answer: why this country, why now, what DBIA brings |
| Stakeholder email | 150–250 words | Direct, respectful, specific | One ask per email. Name the recipient's specific interest. |
| WhatsApp broadcast | Under 100 words | Conversational, action-oriented | Emoji acceptable. Clear link. One action only. |
