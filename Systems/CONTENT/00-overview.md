# CONTENT — Overview

## Single Responsibility

Turn Carlson's knowledge and ideas into published articles for the DBIA Online Magazine.

## The Problem This Solves

Carlson knows things that investors, entrepreneurs, and policy makers need to read. He does not write easily. He speaks well. He thinks clearly. He is not sitting down to write a 1,000-word article unprompted.

CONTENT closes that gap. It extracts the idea from a voice note, a paragraph, or a conversation — and turns it into a finished article in his voice.

## Three-Step Process

```
01-voice-extraction → 02-article-structure → 03-publish-checklist
```

| Step | File | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| 01 | `Instructions/01-voice-extraction.md` | Extract the idea and angle. Get what GRIOT needs to write. |
| 02 | `Instructions/02-article-structure.md` | Structure and write the full draft in Carlson's voice. |
| 03 | `Instructions/03-publish-checklist.md` | Review, approve, and prepare for publication. |

**No draft is written before Step 01 is complete. No article is published before Step 03 is complete.**

## What CONTENT Produces

- Feature articles for the DBIA Online Magazine (1,000–1,500 words)
- Webinar takeaway articles (500–800 words, one per webinar)
- Country intelligence briefs (structured 600-word summaries for magazine)
- Op-eds and thought leadership pieces under Carlson's byline

## Voice Reference

`Data/References/carlson-voice.md` — the specification for how Carlson sounds on paper. Read this before writing any draft.

## Article Template

`Data/Templates/dbia-magazine-article.md` — the standard structure for DBIA Magazine pieces.
