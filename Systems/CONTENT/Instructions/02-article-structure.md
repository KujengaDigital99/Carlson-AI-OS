# Step 02 — Article Structure and Draft

## Purpose

Using the four extracted elements from Step 01, write a full article in Carlson's voice.

## Before Writing: Read These First

- `Data/References/carlson-voice.md` — his voice specification
- `Data/Templates/dbia-magazine-article.md` — the magazine structure

## Standard Article Structures

### Feature Articles (1,000–1,500 words)

| Section | Length | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | 8–12 words | Specific, active, not clickbait. Names the thing. |
| Opening | 100–150 words | Drop the reader into the room, the moment, the problem. Not context-setting. |
| The diagnosis | 200–300 words | What is actually happening here — the systemic reality, not just the symptom. |
| The evidence | 200–300 words | The specific story, person, event, or data that proves it. |
| The DBIA lens | 150–200 words | How DBIA or AEM connects to this. Not a sales pitch — a contribution. |
| The call | 100–150 words | What should the reader do, think, or decide? Concrete. Directional. |

### Webinar Takeaway Articles (500–800 words)

| Section | Length | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | 8–12 words | e.g. "What Rwanda's Investors Are Actually Looking For" |
| Opening | 60–80 words | What the webinar surfaced that surprised even Carlson |
| Three key takeaways | 100–150 words each | The real intelligence from the session |
| Next step | 60–80 words | What readers should do (register, attend next webinar) |

### Country Intelligence Briefs (600 words)

| Section | Length | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Country headline | 1 sentence | The investment story in one sentence |
| Why now | 100 words | The specific window — policy, infrastructure, demand |
| Priority sectors | 150 words | 2–3 sectors with specific evidence |
| Entry points | 150 words | How DBIA enters — country partner, embassy, DFI anchor |
| Risk landscape | 100 words | Honest. Not alarmist. Not naive. |

## Voice Rules (from `carlson-voice.md`)

- Active voice throughout
- Africa-confident — not apologetic, not over-explaining for a Western audience
- Specific over general — a name, a number, a date always beats a vague claim
- Warm but not sentimental
- Never: "Africa is a continent full of opportunity" — that is noise

## After the Draft

Send the draft to Carlson with:
"Read this out loud. If any sentence doesn't sound like something you'd say in a room, mark it. We revise until it does."

Proceed to Step 03 only after Carlson confirms the draft sounds like him.
