# Step 03 — Publish Checklist

## Purpose

Ensure every article is ready before it goes into the DBIA Online Magazine.

## The Checklist

### Voice Check
- [ ] Carlson has read the draft out loud
- [ ] Every sentence sounds like something he would say in a room
- [ ] No generic Africa narrative tropes ("the next frontier", "amazing continent", etc.)
- [ ] Tone is warm and authoritative — not academic, not NGO-passive

### Accuracy Check
- [ ] Every person named: name, title, organisation confirmed correct
- [ ] Every number or statistic is sourced (event attendance, investment figures, dates)
- [ ] Any country data (regulations, market size, sector stats) has been verified

### DBIA Brand Check
- [ ] Carlson's byline is correct: "Carlson Ifughe, General Secretary — Africa Entrepreneurship Masterminds / Doing Business in Africa"
- [ ] DBIA is described correctly (not as an NGO, not as a consulting firm)
- [ ] AEM is described correctly (not as a training programme)
- [ ] If 80eight or any partner is mentioned, their description is accurate

### Legal / Sensitivity Check
- [ ] No language that implies DBIA provides financial advice
- [ ] No forward-looking statements about investment returns
- [ ] Nothing that could embarrass a named person or organisation
- [ ] Cross-border investment opportunities described as "indicative" not "guaranteed"

### Publication Ready
- [ ] Headline is 8–12 words, specific, active
- [ ] Spell-checked
- [ ] Author bio / byline confirmed
- [ ] Article tagged with relevant categories (Country, Sector, Event, Date)
- [ ] Image or header graphic confirmed (or flagged for design)

## Where Articles Go

| Type | Destination |
|---|---|
| Feature articles | DBIA Online Magazine — under Carlson's byline |
| Webinar takeaways | DBIA Online Magazine — tagged to the country webinar |
| Country intelligence briefs | DBIA Online Magazine — country-tagged |
| Op-eds | DBIA Online Magazine + Carlson's LinkedIn |

## After Publication

1. LinkedIn post — 3-sentence tease + link
2. WhatsApp broadcast — short version to relevant country/investor group
3. Email to webinar registrants (if webinar-related)
4. File the article in `Knowledge/DBIA/Magazine/` with date and category tags
