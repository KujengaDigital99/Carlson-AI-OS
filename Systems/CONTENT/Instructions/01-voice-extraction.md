# Step 01 — Voice Extraction

## Purpose

Before any article is drafted, GRIOT must extract:
1. The core idea (what is this article actually saying?)
2. The angle (what makes it specific to Carlson's experience?)
3. The evidence (what specific story, event, or data grounds it?)
4. The audience (who is reading this, and what do they need from it?)

Without these four things, the draft will be generic. Generic does not serve DBIA's credibility.

## How to Run This Step

Carlson gives GRIOT a raw input. This can be:
- A voice note (transcribed)
- A paragraph of rough thoughts
- A conversation summary
- A webinar topic he wants to turn into an article
- "I want to write something about [X]"

GRIOT reflects back the four elements and asks Carlson to confirm or correct before proceeding to Step 02.

## The Four Extraction Questions

**1. Core idea:**
"What is the one thing you want the reader to walk away knowing?"

**2. Angle:**
"What's the specific thing that happened — in a room, at an event, in a conversation — that made you want to write about this?"

**3. Evidence:**
"Who else was in that room? What was the outcome? What did someone say that you haven't stopped thinking about?"

**4. Audience:**
"Who is reading this? A diaspora investor in the US who's never been to Rwanda? A South African entrepreneur who knows DBIA? A policy maker at a DFI?"

## What GRIOT Produces at the End of Step 01

A brief (3–5 bullet points) that states:
- The article's core argument in one sentence
- The angle (the specific, lived thing that makes this real)
- The evidence (the concrete story, person, number, or event)
- The target reader and what they need to do or believe after reading

This goes to Carlson for confirmation. If he approves, proceed to Step 02. If not, revise.

## Common Failure Modes — Do Not Proceed to Step 02 With Any of These

- The idea is generic ("Africa has investment opportunities") — push for specificity
- There is no lived experience grounding it — push for a story
- The audience is "everyone" — push for one reader
- Carlson is not sure what he wants to say — stop here and help him decide first
