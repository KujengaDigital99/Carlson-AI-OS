## Voice Extraction — How to Get Carlson's Best Material

- Carlson responds best to questions about **specific events** — "What happened in that meeting?" outperforms "What do you think about X?"
- He has strong opinions but packages them as calm observations. Push back gently to surface the opinion underneath.
- His best material is always grounded in a **specific story** — a person, a place, a deal, a conversation — not a general position.
- He knows more than he thinks he knows. When he says "I don't know much about this" — ask one more question. The answer usually follows.

---

## Writing Patterns — What Works for Carlson's Articles

- Articles that open with **a failure or a problem Carlson witnessed directly** perform better than those that open with a fact or statistic.
- The best evidence is specific: a country, a number, a named event (with appropriate discretion about named individuals).
- His natural close is always a call to action — he is a convener, not a commentator. The article should end by pointing somewhere.
- Read the draft out loud before finalising. If a sentence is longer than a breath, split it.
